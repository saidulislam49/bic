The way Muhammad Rasulullah saw. loved.

PUBLISHED: 6 March, 2021

Category: Islam

Kindness, welfare, compassion and love are the great virtues of human beings. There is no human being who does not have kindness, compassion and love in his mind. Human love for human beings is an innate thing. God has created pure human beings with the quality of human love for human beings. That's why people love people. People pity people. But the one who is the greatest of human beings, whom Allah has sent for pure human welfare, has made it a source of kindness, compassion and love - the extent of his love and compassion towards human beings is unimaginable. Who loved the rich and the poor. Women and men too. Plants, animals and every living thing. Whose love was unconditional, unadulterated for all and sundry. He is our beloved Prophet Muhammad (peace be upon him).

How was his love for people, how anxious and restless he was for the welfare of the people, how he loved his relatives, people far away. I will try to talk a little bit about the way he loved - InshaAllah.

Nabiji sa. He called upon all human beings with a voice full of love, compassion and affection to lead them in the true and right path. He has destroyed himself to such an extent that Allah Ta'ala himself has revealed the verse, ‘O Prophet, I have not sent down the Qur'an on you to harm you.’ [Surah Ta Ha: 2]

He himself has suffered hundreds of hardships. The mountain of suffering has risen in the book. Even then, no human being should be burnt in the fire of hell in any way, this was the constant wish of the compassionate prophet. Such was his selfless love for man. Because after the cruel torture on him in the field of Taif, when the angel of the mountains called to the Prophet (peace and blessings of Allaah be upon him) and saluted him, saying, ‘O Muhammad! God Almighty has seen all that your people have done to you. I am the angel of the mountains. My Lord has sent me to you, that you may instruct me in them. So what do you want? If you wish, I will impose the two great mountains of [Mecca] on them. ' He said, "Do not do such a thing. Rather, I hope that Allah, the Exalted, will bring forth from their backs a person who will worship one God and will not associate anyone with Him." [Bukhari: 3231]

Explaining the mercy of the Messenger to the Ummah, Allah the Almighty said, "A Messenger has come to you from among you who is afflicted with your pain. He is your benefactor.
